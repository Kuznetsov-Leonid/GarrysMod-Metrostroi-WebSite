81-717.5/714.5

Short description

They differ from previous models not only by the exterior of the cab of the head car, but also by more powerful engines, the absence of driver's cabs in intermediate cars, and also by the wider use of electronics in control devices. Structurally, they represent an intermediate link from type "E" cars to type "I" and have many different modifications. In the metro of a number of cities (Baku, Kiev, Minsk, Moscow, Novosibirsk and so on), models 81-717 / 714 and their modifications carry out the bulk of traffic.

Detailed description

Car 81-717 / 714 is a type of electric subway cars developed and produced by the Mytishchi Machine-Building Plant (since 1976) and the Leningrad Carriage-Building Plant named after V.I. I.E. Egorova (from 1980 to 1995). Cars 81-717 are head cars, and 81-714 are intermediate; all cars are motorized. They are known as the 81st series, but this nickname is fundamentally incorrect, because the digital index "81" is used not only in the designation of all metro cars produced in the Russian Federation (in Prague, Baku and Tbilisi after overhaul in 1996-2011 - 81-71M), but the model itself is, in principle, not the “81st type of carriage produced” (starting with the first car of type “A” produced by the Mytishchi machine-building plant). Most of these car models are known under the nickname "Numbered", since this is the only model that has ever existed, used, existing and used in passenger operation (except 81-718 / 719), which does not have any type letter, only digital name. This, in turn, is due to the fact that this type of cars - 81-717 / 714 - was originally intended as a temporary one - for testing a piece of equipment that was later going to be used on models planned to be more advanced for that time - cars of types 81-715.1 /716.1 "I" and their modifications. However, due to the fact that their production was delayed, and the tests of those already released revealed many shortcomings, and even at a time when the Soviet subways faced an acute shortage of rolling stock, it was decided to launch Numbered into mass production.
